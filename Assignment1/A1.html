Clean

Remove any outliers that does not have at least 1 read per million in n of the samples. Denoting n as the smallest group of replicates which is the control group of 53. Using n = 53 conduct the removal of low counts.

#translate out counts into counts per millison using 
#the edgeR package function cpm
cpms = cpm(geneData[,2:485])
rownames(cpms) <- geneData[,1]
# get rid of low counts
keep = rowSums(cpms >1) >=53
geneData_exp_filtered = geneData[keep,]
nrow(geneData) - nrow(geneData_exp_filtered)

Important notes about code idea The normalization was done with only the 20 randomly sampled data because a boxplot, and density plot with over 400 samples was too chaotic. The sample takes 10 control, and 10 case randomly from the whole dataset. Because of this only 20 samples were normalized for the analysis of plots.

What are the control and test conditions of the dataset? The control is the NEG columns in the dataset that are not effected by SARS-COV-2, and the test is the POS are patients with SARS-COV-2 positive results from nasopharyngeal swabs.

Why is the dataset of interest to you? My current BCB330Y1 research is about genetic association studies with SARS-Cov-2. I wanted to learn more about the topic, and use my time efficiently to expand my knowledge in this area.

Were there expression values that were not unique for specific genes? How did you handle these? There were no cases of expression values that were not unique for specific genes. However there were cases where there were multiple Ensemblid for a single gene id given in the raw data. Took care of this by only keeping one Ensemblid and discarding the rest.

Were there expression values that could not be mapped to current HUGO symbols? There were about 1000 genes that did not map to HUGO symbols however out of 20,000 genes that passed the initial filtering process it is not significant.

How many outliers were removed? 14855 genes were removed with low counts that removed genes without at least 1 read per million in the sample.

How did you handle replicates? Replicates in the control and test conditions were all used in the data. However a majority were cut in the low counts filtering process.

What is the final coverage of your dataset? The final dataset has 19291 genes, with 484 samples.53 control, and 431 case samples.
